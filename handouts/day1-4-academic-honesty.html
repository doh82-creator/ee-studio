Academic Honesty Checklist

Handout 4 · Day 1 — Orientation  |  Name: _______________________
Academic malpractice in an EE can result in the loss of the diploma point, or worse. This is not just a rule to avoid — understanding it well protects the integrity of your own research and argument.
Before You Start Researching
  • I understand the difference between quoting, paraphrasing, and summarizing — and that all three require citation.
  • I know which citation style my school requires (e.g., MLA, APA, Chicago) and will use it consistently.
  • I understand the difference between legitimate collaboration (discussing ideas) and collusion (submitting shared/copied work).
  • I will keep a working bibliography from Day 1 onward, not just at the end.
While Researching & Writing
  • Every direct quote is in quotation marks with a source and page/paragraph reference.
  • Every paraphrased idea is cited, even if I changed all the wording.
  • Data, quotes, or interview responses in my essay are real and unaltered.
  • I keep drafts and research notes as evidence of my own process (useful for the RPPF too).
  • If I use any AI tool for brainstorming, translation, or editing, I acknowledge it according to my school's and the IB's current policy.
Common Pitfalls
  • Patchwriting — changing a few words of a source without truly rephrasing the idea
  • Forgetting to cite facts/statistics because they "feel like common knowledge"
  • Reusing your own work from another IA/assignment without acknowledgment (self-plagiarism)
  • Leaving citation formatting until the very last night
Policies on citation style and the acknowledgment of AI tool use can vary by school and may be updated by the IB. Confirm the current requirement with your EE coordinator before you submit.
