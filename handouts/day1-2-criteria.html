Understanding the Assessment Criteria

Handout 2 · Day 1 — Orientation  |  Name: _______________________
Your EE is marked out of 34 points across five criteria. Knowing them now — before you even choose a topic — helps you make sharper decisions at every later stage of this course.
The Five Criteria
CriterionMarksWhat it really rewardsIn my own words, I should…
AFocus and Method6 A clear, appropriately scoped research question and a method that fits it.
BKnowledge and Understanding6 Subject-specific knowledge and terminology used accurately and in context.
CCritical Thinking12 Analysis, evaluation, and discussion of evidence — not just description of it.
DPresentation4 Structure, academic formatting, and consistent citation throughout.
EEngagement6 Genuine reflection on your research process, recorded in the RPPF.
Notice: Criterion C (Critical Thinking) alone is worth as much as A + B combined. The single biggest reason students lose marks is writing that describes evidence instead of analyzing it. We will return to this distinction constantly over the next 10 days.
