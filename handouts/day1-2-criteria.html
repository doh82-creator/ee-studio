Understanding the Assessment Criteria

Handout 2 · Day 1 — Orientation  |  Name: _______________________
Starting with this cohort, your EE is marked under the 2027 assessment model — 30 points across five criteria (A–D apply to the essay itself; E applies to your reflective statement on the RPF). Knowing them now — before you even choose a topic — helps you make sharper decisions at every later stage of this course.
The Five Criteria
CriterionMarksWhat it really rewardsIn my own words, I should…
AFramework for the Essay6 Whether your research question, method, and structure genuinely support the research — not just "correct formatting."
BKnowledge & Understanding6 Choosing credible, relevant sources and using subject-specific terms and concepts accurately.
CAnalysis & Line of Argument6 Interpreting evidence rather than just describing it, with a clear, continuous argument from question to conclusion.
DDiscussion & Evaluation8 Discussing what your findings mean, considering other viewpoints, and evaluating the strengths and limits of your own research.
EReflection4 Evaluating how the EE process changed you as a learner — not a log of what you did. Written as your final reflective statement (max 500 words) on the RPF.
Notice: Criterion D (Discussion & Evaluation) carries the most marks of any single criterion — 8 points, more than A or B alone. The biggest reason students lose marks is stopping at what they found, without discussing what it means or how far it can be trusted. We will return to this distinction constantly over the next 10 days.
