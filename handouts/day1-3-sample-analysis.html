Sample Excerpt — Economics EE
Subject: Economics  ·  Word count of excerpt: ~210 (Introduction only)
The Effect of a Minimum Wage Increase on Youth Part-Time Employment in the Seoul Retail Sector
Research Question

"To what extent did the 2018–2019 minimum wage increase affect the number of part-time positions offered to workers aged 15–24 in small retail businesses (fewer than 5 employees) in Seoul?"

Between 2018 and 2019, South Korea's statutory minimum wage rose by 29%, one of the sharpest increases among OECD economies in a single two-year period. Public debate at the time centered on two competing predictions: that higher wages would improve living standards for low-income youth workers, or that small business owners would respond by cutting part-time hours altogether. National employment statistics show a decline in part-time positions among workers aged 15–24 during this period, but aggregate figures obscure significant variation between sectors and business sizes. Small retail businesses — convenience stores, cafés, and independent shops — are widely cited in Korean media as the businesses most affected, yet few studies isolate this specific segment. This essay investigates whether, and to what extent, the minimum wage increase is associated with reduced part-time hiring specifically within small retail businesses in Seoul, using a combination of labor statistics analysis and structured interviews with small business owners.

Analysis Worksheet
  1. Underline the Research Question above. Is it focused, analytical, and answerable within 4,000 words? What makes it so (or not)?
  2. Circle 3 subject-specific terms/concepts already visible in this short excerpt. What does their presence signal about Criterion B?
  3. What is the "problem" or "gap" that motivates this research? (Hint: look for the sentence that says something is missing from existing knowledge.)
  4. What method is hinted at in the final sentence? What kind of evidence will this essay likely use?
  5. On a scale of 1 (purely descriptive) to 5 (highly analytical), how would you rate this topic's potential for Critical Thinking (Criterion C)? Justify your rating.
